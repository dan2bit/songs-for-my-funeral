## Core Identity

This is primarily a **live blues and roots music** collection. The center of gravity
is electric blues guitar — specifically the American tradition running from Chicago
and Texas through to its contemporary practitioners — but the orbit is wide enough to
include acoustic blues, Americana, soul, roots rock, and artists who borrow
meaningfully from those traditions. The unifying thread is not genre label but a set
of values: **musicianship, authenticity, instrumental voice, and the feeling of a
room where something real is happening**.

This is a listener who goes to shows alone, sits close, brings autograph books, and
has seen some artists ten-plus times. The collection is about sustained attention, not
sampling.

## What Gets Bought vs. Passed

### Strong buy signals:
- **Seen before** at any level (headliner or support) → near-automatic for local shows
- **Small intimate room** + right artist → often the decisive factor
- **Acoustic blues / roots storytelling** format (Lyle Lovett, Dave Alvin/Gilmore duo,
  Jake Xerxes Fussell) at the right venue
- **Birchmere specifically** — extremely high show-to-buy ratio across history
- **Rams Head On Stage** — close second; seated, intimate, consistent
- **VIP packages** for top-tier artists — purchased without hesitation; the strongest
  single signal of artist importance in the collection (Billy Gibbons, ZZ Ward,
  The Lone Bellow, Robert Jon & The Wreck)

### Observed pattern worth noting:
The show history skews heavily toward **female-led and female-fronted acts** —
Popović, Venable, Venson, Foley, Collier, Taylor, Nicole, Ward, McLachlan, Russell,
Larkin Poe, Mohini Dey, and others. This is a real pattern but should be understood
as a *consequence* of following the blues and roots tradition, where a disproportionate
share of the most compelling contemporary voices happen to be women, rather than a
filter applied at the point of purchase. The hat autograph category is deliberately
female-only, which is a more conscious curatorial choice.

### Frequent passes:
- **Arena-scale shows** — outside interest even for familiar artists
- **Tribute acts / cover bands** — essentially never buys
- **Pure comedy** — not tracked here
- **Dinner-theater / supper club formats** where the show is secondary to the meal
- **Outdoor festivals** unless specific anchor acts are very strong
- **High show density** — three shows in four nights is at or past the fatigue threshold,
  especially when aggregate travel distance compounds (e.g., two Annapolis nights plus
  a DC night in a single week)

## Live Music Values

### Venue preferences (strongest to weakest):
1. **The Birchmere** — gold standard. Intimate, seated, attentive audience, doors at
   5pm, food service. Show-to-buy ratio across history is exceptional.
2. **Rams Head On Stage** (Annapolis) — seated tables, close sightlines, similar feel
   to Birchmere. Drives to Annapolis routinely.
3. **Hamilton Live** (DC) — GA Gold Seated preferred when available. Right downtown,
   $13 parking.
4. **9:30 Club** (DC) — GA standing. IMP flagship. Higher energy, younger crowd.
5. **Wolf Trap Filene Center** — reserved pavilion seats only (not lawn). Buys paper
   tickets at box office. A-list headliners justify the premium.
6. **Wolf Trap Barns** — intimate acoustic room; very occasional.
7. **Jammin' Java** (Vienna) — small, local, good for emerging artists.
8. **Union Stage / Pearl Street / Howard Theatre** — Union Stage Presents umbrella;
   varied; case-by-case.
9. **Hub City Vinyl** (Hagerstown, MD) — small DIY room; niche blues/Americana acts
   worth a drive for the right artist.
10. **Collective Encore** (Columbia, MD) — supper club; seen Vanessa Collier twice here.

### Prefers:
- **Intimate rooms** where you can see the artist's hands and face clearly
- **Artists who play their own instruments** — guitar especially
- **Autograph-seekable artists** — brings RHBS (Red House Blues Signatures) and APS
  books to shows; hat signing eligibility limited to female musicians
- **Listening audiences** rather than background-music crowds
- **Arrive early, get a good spot** — typically near the front
- **Show is the whole evening** — not pre-gaming or post-show plans competing
- **Seated vs. standing** is not a strong preference in itself — it follows naturally
  from venue choice rather than driving it

### Friction factors (things that suppress ticket purchases):
- **Show density** — the primary friction variable is the number of shows in a rolling
  window combined with aggregate travel distance. Weekday vs. weekend is not the
  relevant axis; what matters is how many nights in a row and how far the drives are.
  Three shows in four nights becomes three shows in four nights plus two Annapolis
  trips, which is the actual load being weighed.
- **Long drives** — 45–60 min acceptable; 90+ min requires a strong pull
- **High ticket cost** — noted and tracked; $100+ shows get explicit merch restraint
- **Arena format** — almost never buys regardless of artist
- **Late start times** — relevant mainly when the next day's commitments are affected

## Artist Discovery — External Curated Sources

The following external sources add meaningful weight when an artist appears on them,
even if the event itself (cruise, festival, awards show) would not be attended. Presence
on these rosters is treated as a credibility signal, not an attendance trigger.

**Blues cruises** (artist rosters surface new names):
- Keeping the Blues Alive at Sea (Joe Bonamassa, annual)
- Rock Legends Cruise
- Legendary Rhythm & Blues Cruise (LRBC)
- Big Blues Bender (Las Vegas, late Sep)

**Festival lineups** (roster research, not attendance):
- Springing the Blues (Jacksonville Beach, FL) — already sourced Toronzo Cannon here
- Hardly Strictly Bluegrass (San Francisco, early Oct)
- Americanafest / AmericanaFest (Nashville, Sep)
- Stagecoach (Indio CA, April — country/Americana crossover)
- Telluride Bluegrass (Colorado, June)
- All Good Now Festival (DC/MD area — this one sometimes gets attended directly)

**Awards nominees and winners**:
- Blues Music Awards (Blues Foundation) — nominees signal who is being recognized
  within the professional blues community; winners even more so
- Americana Music Association Awards — similar function for the Americana side

In practice: an artist appearing on a BMA nominee list or a Keeping the Blues Alive
cruise roster who also shows up in the DC area is a much stronger recommendation
candidate than one sourced only from a venue newsletter.

## Genre Map

### Buys consistently:
- Electric blues (Chicago, Texas, contemporary)
- Blues-rock (guitar-forward, not pop-crossover)
- Acoustic/Delta blues
- Roots rock / Americana (when artist-forward, not festival-background)
- Soul/R&B when instrumentally grounded (not pure pop production)
- Singer-songwriter with strong guitar voice (Fussell, Lovett, Gilmore)
- Cosmic country / psychedelic country (Donato)
- Bluegrass when adventurous (Greensky, Lone Bellow adjacent)

### Evaluates case-by-case:
- Jazz-adjacent (if the guitarist is the draw — Mancuso, Posen)
- Folk (if the songwriting is strong — Hansard, Fussell)
- Rock (if the blues influence is primary — ZZ Ward)
- Jam band (Greensky, Wood Brothers — yes; generic jam — no)

### Rarely or never buys:
- Pop-country
- Hip-hop
- EDM / electronic
- Classical (separate from this tracking)
- Comedy (separate calendar)
- Tribute/cover bands
- Arena rock regardless of pedigree

## Artist Relationship Signals

- **VIP packages** — the strongest single indicator of how much an artist matters.
  Purchased selectively: Billy Gibbons (birthday milestone), ZZ Ward, The Lone Bellow,
  Robert Jon & The Wreck. Each represents a deliberate decision to invest beyond the
  ticket in the artist relationship.
- **Post-show accessibility** — a complementary but secondary signal. Artists who stay
  and meet fans after sets are noted and appreciated; this adds to the relationship but
  is not the primary driver. Tracked via Artist Interaction column (Autograph / Photo /
  Both) in show history.
- **Autograph books** (RHBS and APS) are brought to shows with intent — autographs
  are pursued selectively, not compulsively
- **Hat autographs** — female musicians only; a more deliberate and curated category
- **Post-show notes** sent by email after attended shows — the archive is a genuine
  personal record, not just logistics
