# Songs for my Funeral — Slideshow Guide

*For whoever ends up doing this. Thank you for doing this.*

---

## What you have

Someone — probably me, in a better season — put together a playlist of 26 songs organized into six chapters. There's a slideshow to go with it. Your job, if you choose to accept it, is to fill it with photos and run it at the service.

This document tells you everything you need to know. You don't need any special software beyond PowerPoint (or Google Slides, in a pinch). You don't need to be technical. Take it one track at a time.

## How the slideshow is structured

There are 85 slides in three types. You only need to touch one of them.

**Chapter cards** (6 slides — one per chapter)
These have the chapter number, title, and an epigraph. They're meant to stay exactly as they are. Don't edit them.

**Track cards** (26 slides — one per song)
These show the track number, title, performer, a lyric, and the album cover. They're meant to stay exactly as they are.

**Photo slides** (52 slides — two templates per track, one landscape, one portrait)
**These are the ones you edit.** Each photo slide has a dark placeholder image with "LANDSCAPE PHOTO" or "PORTRAIT / SQUARE PHOTO" on it. You replace that placeholder with your photos using the Change Picture workflow described below.

The two templates exist because photos come in different shapes:
- Use the **landscape** template for wide photos (phone landscape, old prints, group shots)
- Use the **portrait/square** template for tall or square photos (phone portrait, school photos, headshots)

You can use as many photo slides per track as you like — just duplicate the right template and swap in a photo. More on that below.

## How many photos per track

Each track slide has a note in the **speaker notes** (the panel below the slide in PowerPoint) telling you the track duration and a suggested photo count. To see them: View → Notes, or just look at the panel below each slide.

It will say something like:

> `4:51 · 36 photos @ 8s each · range: 29–48 photos (10s–6s per slide)`

This means:
- The track is 4 minutes 51 seconds long
- If you want each photo visible for about **8 seconds**, use **36 photos**
- If you want a slower, more meditative pace (10 seconds each), use **29 photos**
- If you want a brisker pace (6 seconds each), use **48 photos**

There's no wrong answer. For a memorial, slower tends to feel more right. If you're short on photos for a particular track, fewer slides at a longer hold time works just as well.

## Adding photos — step by step

### 1. Gather your photos

Sort or loosely group them by mood or era if you can — it doesn't need to be chronological, but it helps to have an idea of what goes where before you start. Chapter themes are:

| Chapter | Tracks | Theme |
|---------|--------|-------|
| 1 — I Am Where I'm Meant to Be | 1–3 | Arrival, peace |
| 2 — You'll Be Okay (Grief Sucks) | 4–9 | Comfort, community |
| 3 — Don't Worry About Me | 10–11 | Release |
| 4 — You're Still in the Pink | 12–18 | Celebration, joy |
| 5 — But Before We Part | 19–23 | Tenderness |
| 6 — Go in Good Graces | 24–26 | Farewell |

### 2. Find the photo slides

In the slide panel on the left, scroll to find the landscape or portrait template for the track you're working on. Each one comes right after its track card. The track number and title are in the footer at the bottom.

### 3. Duplicate the template

**Right-click** the template slide in the left panel → **Duplicate Slide**. Do this once for each photo you plan to use for that track. If you need 10 photos, make 10 duplicates. You can always add or delete more later.

Keep the original template at the end of the group — it makes it easy to duplicate again if you need more.

### 4. Replace the placeholder with your photo

Each photo slide contains a placeholder image. Because it's a real image (not a box), PowerPoint lets you swap it in place using **Change Picture** — no deleting or repositioning needed.

**To replace the placeholder:**
1. Click once on the placeholder image to select it
2. Right-click → **Change Picture** → **From a File…** (or "From This Device…")
3. Browse to your photo and select it
4. PowerPoint replaces the placeholder with your photo, fitted to the same position and size

The photo fills the frame automatically. If the crop isn't right, right-click the image → **Format Picture** → **Crop** to adjust what's visible without resizing the frame.

**Note:** Change Picture is the key step. It keeps the photo in exactly the right position and size for the slide layout, so you don't have to fiddle with placement at all.

### 5. Choosing landscape vs. portrait template

Look at your photo before you decide which template to use:
- Wider than it is tall → landscape template
- Taller than it is wide, or roughly square → portrait template

It's fine to use only one type for a whole track if all your photos are the same orientation.

### 6. Set slide timing (optional but helpful)

If you want the slideshow to advance automatically without someone clicking through:

**PowerPoint:** Select all photo slides for a track → Transitions tab → uncheck "On Mouse Click" → check "After" → set the number of seconds. Use the suggested timing from the speaker notes.

If you'd rather advance manually on the day, skip this entirely. Manual is fine — it gives you more control.

## Running it on the day

This is the most important part. The music and the slideshow run separately — you play the audio from one app and run the slides from another, on the same laptop. They don't sync automatically, and that's fine. You just start them at the same time.

### What you need

- A laptop
- An HDMI cable to connect to a TV or projector
- The laptop set to **extend** display (not mirror), so you can see your speaker notes privately while the audience sees the slides
- Speakers, or the TV/venue's audio system connected to the laptop's headphone jack or via Bluetooth

### Setup (do this before people arrive)

1. Connect the laptop to the TV or projector via HDMI
2. Open `slides-songs-for-my-funeral.pptx` in PowerPoint
3. Open `songs-for-my-funeral.mp3` in VLC, Apple Music, Windows Media Player, or any music app — whatever you have
4. Put the music app in the background, with the playlist queued and paused at the start
5. Start the slideshow: Slideshow → From Beginning (or press F5). It should appear full-screen on the TV.
6. On your laptop screen you'll see your speaker notes, including the timing suggestions

### Starting the show

When you're ready to begin:
1. Press **Space** or **→** to advance the slideshow to the first real slide
2. Immediately switch to the music app and press **Play**
3. Advance through slides manually as the music plays

Don't worry about perfect sync. The slides are not timed to the second — they're meant to accompany the music, not be locked to it. If you fall behind, advance a few slides quickly. If you're ahead, just wait.

### If something goes wrong

- **Wrong slide:** Press **←** to go back, or type a slide number and press Enter to jump
- **Music skips:** Pause, re-queue the track, unpause
- **Someone needs a moment:** Press **B** to black out the screen. Press **B** again to bring it back.
- **Everything is fine, actually:** Most people won't notice minor timing mismatches. They're watching the photos and listening to the music. Give yourself permission to be imperfect.

### Using chapter files instead of the master

If you'd rather have more control — pausing between chapters, for example — play the six chapter mp3 files instead of the master. Start each one when the corresponding chapter card appears on screen. The chapter cards are the title slides between groups of tracks.
